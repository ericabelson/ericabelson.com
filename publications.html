Popular & Professional Writing

Essays

  • Finding Earth Engine Data Doesn’t Have to Be a Struggle: Let AI Agents Do the Heavy Lifting – Use AI agents to search Google Earth Engine datasets instead of manually browsing cryptic collection pages.
  • Building an Agentic Scientific Research Team with Google’s Agent Development Kit – Build a multi-agent research team using Google’s Agent Development Kit to automate literature review and methodology extraction.

Posts

  • M-PACE: Multimodal GenAI Compliance – Evaluate multimodal LLM compliance for vision and text capabilities, balancing accuracy, cost, and latency.
  • Teaching GenAI to think before speaking – Safety-aware chain-of-thought and Monte Carlo Tree Search help LLMs reason through multiple paths before responding.
  • Jagged Frontier – Part 3: Blind Trust at the Edge – How direct LLM output copying (“retainment”) signals success within the jagged frontier but leads to errors outside it.
  • Jagged Frontier – Part 2 – Introduction to Centaur and Cyborg collaboration styles—distinct vs. blended human-AI teamwork patterns.
  • Jagged Frontier - part 1 – The jagged frontier concept: the unpredictable boundary between what AI handles well and poorly.
  • Author-affiliations: A Python Tool for NSF Proposal Writers – Automate NSF COA document creation by pulling author and affiliation metadata from Crossref and OpenAlex.
  • AI and Academic Writing: Ethical Integrity – Propose including AI prompt logs in academic publishing to support ethical transparency and reduce language barriers.
  • AI and Accessibility in Academia: A Step Toward Inclusion – GenAI technologies like LLMs and vectorization reduce barriers for disabled and non-native English-speaking researchers.
  • AI and Mitigating Bias in Academia – How GenAI both introduces and mitigates bias in academic writing, with focus on responsible tool use.
